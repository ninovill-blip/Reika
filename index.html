My Approach

Here's what I do differently: Before I even think about picking up a syringe, I sit with you. I mean really sit with you—under proper lighting, no rushing, no pressure. During my days on stage and in front of cameras, I learned that faces tell stories in angles, shadows, and proportions. Now, as your injector, I read those stories clinically.

My consultation process is thorough because I won't compromise. I'll photograph your face from five angles. I'll assess your facial thirds, your skeletal structure, how your muscles move when you smile or frown. I examine skin thickness with my fingers—yes, I actually touch and feel before I treat. I check for asymmetries you might not even notice. I ask about your medical history, medications, previous treatments. And I listen. In my vast experience, the best results come when I understand not just your face, but your life.

Then comes my non-negotiable standard: I only use FDA-approved products, and I examine every single vial before opening it. I check the lot number against the manufacturer's database. I inspect for clarity—no cloudiness, no particles. I verify the seal hasn't been tampered with. I confirm it's been stored at the correct temperature. If anything seems even slightly off, I won't use it. Period. Your face deserves nothing less than pharmaceutical-grade precision.

I won't downplay that injectables can sting—some areas more than others. But I'll most likely distract you with conversation about your goals, your concerns, maybe even stories from my acting days when I learned firsthand how millimeters matter on camera. My technique is deliberate: slow injection, constant visualization, strategic placement. I've refined this over thousands of faces.

This is how I work. This is my standard. And I wouldn't have it any other way.

Lips

"The beauty of a woman must be seen from in her eyes, because that is the doorway to her heart, the place where love resides." — Audrey Hepburn

Here's how I approach lips: First, I have you smile. Then I watch how your lips move, where they thin, how much tooth shows. I measure your philtrum-to-chin ratio—it matters for proportion. I assess your lip architecture: the vermillion border definition, cupid's bow shape, the wet-dry junction. I check for asymmetries between upper and lower lips. All of this before I even discuss product.

When we do treat, I'll most likely use Juvéderm Ultra XC or Restylane Kysse—both FDA-approved hyaluronic acid fillers that I've personally tested and trusted over hundreds of treatments. These come pre-mixed with lidocaine, but I won't lie to you: you'll feel it. There's no way around the initial pinch. But my technique minimizes discomfort—I inject slowly, I talk you through it, and I use ice before and after.

My injection pattern is methodical: I start at the vermillion border, ensuring sharp definition first. Then I add volume strategically—usually the body of the lip, avoiding overfilling the white roll which creates that unnatural "duck lip" look. I'm constantly stepping back, checking symmetry from multiple angles. In my acting days, I learned cameras don't lie about asymmetry. Neither do mirrors. So I get it right.

Each vial gets opened in front of you. You'll see me check the seal, verify the lot number, inspect for clarity. This transparency isn't just professional—it's respectful. You deserve to know exactly what's going in your face.

FDA-Approved Juvéderm Ultra XC, Restylane Kysse, Restylane Silk

Cheeks

"Elegance is the only beauty that never fades." — Audrey Hepburn

My cheek assessment is architectural: I examine your zygomatic prominence—where your cheekbones naturally sit. I assess the malar fat pads and how they've descended over time. I look at your face in profile to see the true degree of mid-face volume loss. Then I have you smile big so I can see exactly where we need structural support versus surface enhancement. During my stage days, I learned how lighting hits elevated cheekbones. Now I recreate that clinically.

For cheeks, I'll most likely reach for Juvéderm Voluma XC or Restylane Lyft—these are the only FDA-approved fillers with the viscosity and cohesivity for deep, supraperiosteal placement. I won't use fillers designed for lips in your cheeks. That's asking for trouble. Each product is engineered for specific depths and purposes, and I respect that science.

My technique? I use a blunt-tip cannula more often than a needle for cheeks. It causes less trauma, fewer entry points, and dramatically reduces bruising risk. I enter through a tiny opening near your ear, then fan the product along your zygomatic arch. You'll feel pressure, maybe some odd sensations as I'm working—I warn you about this beforehand. I won't downplay the experience, but I will make it as comfortable as possible.

Before I inject anything, I inspect the syringe under light. I'm looking for the characteristic viscosity, the clear gel appearance, the proper label matching what I ordered. Every vial is temperature-checked—these products must stay between 2-8°C until use. I'm obsessive about this because your safety demands it.

FDA-Approved Juvéderm Voluma XC, Restylane Lyft, Radiesse

Jawline

"Beauty begins the moment you decide to be yourself." — Coco Chanel

Jawline work is engineering: I palpate your mandible to feel the bone structure underneath. I assess your masseter muscle bulk—hypertrophic masseters change my approach. I look at your gonial angle (where jaw meets chin) to determine if we need definition, contour, or both. I have you clench your teeth so I can see exactly where your muscles pull. I measure your facial width-to-length ratio. All of this determines whether you're even a good candidate for jawline filler, and if so, precisely where it goes.

Juvéderm Volux XC is my gold standard for jawline—it's the only FDA-approved filler specifically designed for this area, with the exact firmness needed to create definition that lasts. But I won't sugarcoat the experience: the jawline is sensitive. You'll feel every injection. Some people describe it as a deep ache. I warn you about this during consultation because informed consent matters to me.

My technique is slow and methodical. I use a needle, not a cannula, because I need precision here. I place micro-boluses along the mandibular border, creating a straight line from back to front. I inject perpendicular to the jawbone, ensuring the product sits exactly where it needs to for structure. Between each injection point, I massage and mold the product. I'm constantly checking profile view—that's where jawline work really shows.

Every syringe is checked before use. I verify it's been stored correctly—these products degrade if exposed to heat. I check the consistency by expressing a tiny amount—it should flow smoothly, not be runny or clumpy. I verify the concentration matches what I ordered. In my vast experience with hundreds of jawlines, I've learned that cutting corners on quality control isn't worth the risk. Ever.

FDA-Approved Juvéderm Volux XC, Restylane Defyne, Radiesse

Under Eye

"The eyes are the window to the soul." — Traditional Proverb

Under-eye filler is where I'm most selective: Not everyone is a candidate, and I'll tell you if you're not. First, I assess your skin thickness by gently pinching—thin skin shows every imperfection. I check for festoons or malar bags (filler makes these worse, not better). I look at the depth of your tear trough and whether it's caused by volume loss, skin laxity, or both. I assess your skin tone—the Tyndall effect (bluish discoloration) is more visible in lighter skin. I ask about your sleep, allergies, sinus issues—all of this affects under-eye treatment outcomes.

If you're a good candidate, I use Juvéderm Volbella XC or Restylane—FDA-approved and specifically formulated for this delicate area. These have lower viscosity than cheek fillers because under-eye skin is paper-thin. Before I even open the vial, I examine it under bright light, checking for absolute clarity and perfect consistency. Any particulates? That vial doesn't touch your face.

My injection technique is hyper-precise. I use a cannula, not a needle, entering from the cheek and threading underneath. I place tiny micro-droplets—we're talking 0.05ml at a time—in the supraperiosteal plane. Too superficial and you get the Tyndall effect. Too deep and it won't correct the hollowing. I massage constantly, ensuring smooth integration. You'll feel pressure and maybe some weird sensations. I talk you through every moment.

I'll be completely transparent: under-eye filler carries risks. Swelling, bruising, the Tyndall effect, vascular occlusion (rare but serious). I discuss all of this during consultation. And if I think the risks outweigh benefits for your particular anatomy? I'll recommend against treatment. In my vast experience, saying no to the wrong patient is just as important as saying yes to the right one.

FDA-Approved Juvéderm Volbella XC, Restylane, Belotero Balance

Forehead & Glabella

"Simplicity is the ultimate sophistication." — Leonardo da Vinci

Neurotoxin treatment is pure precision: I start by having you make every facial expression—raise eyebrows, frown, squint, smile. I'm not just watching wrinkles; I'm mapping muscle movement patterns. I assess your frontalis (forehead) strength, your procerus and corrugator (the "11s" between brows), even your nasalis if I see bunny lines. I look for asymmetries—maybe one brow sits higher, or one side of your forehead is more active. All of this determines my dosing and placement strategy.

I only use FDA-approved neurotoxins: Botox Cosmetic, Dysport, Xeomin, or Jeuveau—sourced directly from authorized distributors, never third-party sellers. When the vial arrives, I personally verify the hologram seal, check the lot number against the manufacturer's database, and confirm refrigeration logs. These products are shipped on ice for a reason—heat degrades the protein. I'm obsessive about this.

My injection pattern is strategic, not cookie-cutter. For forehead, I'll most likely use 8-12 injection points depending on your muscle mass and movement patterns. For the glabella, usually 5 points in a specific pattern that targets all three muscle groups. I inject perpendicular to the skin, aspirate first (safety step), then deliver a precise dose. You'll feel small pinches—I won't lie about that. But my technique is fast and I talk you through it.

Here's what I tell every neurotoxin patient: Results aren't immediate. You'll start seeing softening in 3-5 days, full results in 10-14 days. The effect lasts 3-4 months on average. And if you hate it? We can dissolve fillers with hyaluronidase, but neurotoxin has to wear off naturally. So I'm conservative on first treatments. We can always add more at your two-week follow-up. I'd rather under-treat than over-treat.

FDA-Approved Botox Cosmetic, Dysport, Xeomin, Jeuveau

Brow Lift

"Beauty is power; a smile is its sword." — John Ray

Brow lift is where art meets medicine: I assess your brow position at rest using anatomical landmarks—your brows should sit at or slightly above your orbital rim. I check for brow ptosis (drooping), especially laterally where aging shows first. I have you raise and lower your brows to see muscle dominance—some people have strong depressors pulling brows down. I look at your face in repose, in conversation, when you're thinking. During my acting days on stage, I learned that brows communicate emotion more than any other facial feature. Now I sculpt them with strategic neurotoxin placement.

My technique for neurotoxin brow lift is subtle but transformative. I inject into the lateral orbicularis oculi (the muscle that pulls brows down) while carefully avoiding the central frontalis (which lifts). This allows the lifting muscles to work unopposed, creating a 2-3mm elevation—enough to open the eyes, not enough to look surprised. The placement has to be exact. Too medial and you get a Spock brow. Too lateral and nothing happens.

I use FDA-approved Botox or Dysport for this—both work beautifully, though I find Dysport diffuses slightly more, which can be advantageous for brow lift. Each vial is verified batch by batch. I check the reconstitution date (neurotoxin should be used within 24 hours of mixing for maximum potency). I verify the dilution ratio is exactly what I specified—this affects spread and duration.

What you'll feel: Small pinches, maybe 4-6 per side. Some people feel a mild headache afterward—I warn you about this. Results develop over 5-7 days. During my vast experience treating hundreds of brows, I've learned that patience wins. This is precision work. And when done right? It's one of the most elegant transformations in aesthetics. Your eyes look brighter, more awake, more you.

FDA-Approved Botox Cosmetic, Dysport, Xeomin
